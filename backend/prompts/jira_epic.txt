In Jira, an **Epic** is a high-level issue type that represents a large body of work. Think of it as a "container" or a "folder" for a major project or feature that is too big to be completed in a single sprint.

Here is a breakdown of what makes an Epic unique:

### 1. The Hierarchy Position
In the standard Jira hierarchy, an Epic sits at the top of the daily work structure:
*   **Epic:** The big goal (e.g., "Build a Mobile App").
*   **Stories/Tasks:** The specific pieces of work that make up the goal (e.g., "Create Login Screen").
*   **Sub-tasks:** The individual steps to finish a story (e.g., "Design login button").

### 2. Key Characteristics
*   **Duration:** Unlike a User Story, which should be finished in one sprint (usually 2 weeks), an Epic typically spans **multiple sprints** or even months.
*   **Scope:** It captures a broad requirement or a strategic initiative. As the team learns more, they add more "Child Issues" (Stories and Tasks) to the Epic.
*   **Tracking:** Jira provides a specific **Epic Report** and a progress bar to show how much of the Epic is completed based on the status of its child issues.

### 3. Why Use Them?
*   **Organization:** They keep your backlog clean by grouping related work together.
*   **Roadmapping:** Epics are the primary building blocks for the **Jira Timeline (Roadmap)**. They allow managers to see high-level progress without getting lost in the technical details of every single task.
*   **Reporting:** They help stakeholders understand when a major feature will be "Done" rather than just seeing a list of disconnected tasks.
